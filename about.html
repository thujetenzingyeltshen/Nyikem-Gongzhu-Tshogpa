About NGT

Institutional Purpose and National Responsibility

Nyikem Gongzhu Tshogpa (NGT) is a distinguished association in Bhutan comprising recipients of the prestigious Nyikem (Red Scarf), an honor granted by His Majesty the King in recognition of dedicated service to the Tsa-Wa-Sum — the King, Country, and People.

Who We Are

A Platform for Experience, Unity, and Continued Service

Established under the Royal Command, the Tshogpa serves as a platform to bring together experienced individuals who have contributed significantly to national development. It promotes unity, mutual support, and continued service to society by utilizing the wisdom, knowledge, and experience of its members.

NGT is committed to strengthening solidarity among its members while also engaging in meaningful initiatives that benefit the community. The association provides support in areas such as guidance, welfare, and social engagement, ensuring that members remain actively connected and continue to contribute to the nation.

Through its activities and values, Nyikem Gongzhu Tshogpa upholds Bhutan's traditions, reinforces national identity, and supports the vision of a harmonious and progressive society.

History

History and Formation

Nyikem Gongzhu Tshogpa (NGT) was established under the Royal Command of His Majesty the King, with the objective of bringing together recipients of the prestigious Nyikem (Red Scarf). The formation of the Tshogpa was formally approved by the Privy Council of Bhutan, recognizing the importance of creating a unified platform for individuals who have rendered distinguished service to the nation.

The Tshogpa was founded to foster a sense of unity, cooperation, and continued engagement among Red Scarf holders. It provides a structured forum through which members can contribute their experience and knowledge toward national development and community well-being.

Since its establishment, NGT has functioned as a respected association dedicated to upholding Bhutanese values, strengthening solidarity among its members, and supporting the vision of serving the Tsa-Wa-Sum — the King, Country, and People.

Leadership

Organizational Structure

The Tshogpa is guided by an Executive Committee elected by its members. The committee is responsible for overseeing activities, managing resources, and ensuring that the objectives of the organization are fulfilled effectively and transparently.
